Fragrant Vietnamese beef noodle soup

Author: Alijku
Recipe type:Entree

This is a quick version of pho – a wonderful soup made with beef or chicken that the Vietnamese eat for dinner, lunch and even breakfast.


Ingredients:

  1. 300g beef fillet, trimmed of sinew
  2. 1tbs olive oil
  3. 200g rice stick noodles
  4. 2 cups (500ml) beef stock
  5. 1 lemongrass stem (white part only), bruised, cut into 6cm lengths
  6. 1 tbs fish sauce
  7. 11/2 cups bean sprouts, ends trimmed
  8. Vietnamese mint leaves, Thai basil leaves and sliced red chilli, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Rub the beef fillet with the olive oil and season with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper. Heat a large frypan over high heat. Add the beef and cook, turning, for 2-3 minutes until seared on all sides and rare in the centre. Remove from pan, cover loosely with foil and set aside.
  2. Place the rice noodles in a large heatproof bowl and pour over enough boiling water to cover. Leave to soften for 5 minutes, then rinse and drain well.
  3. Place the stock, lemongrass, fish sauce and 2 cups (500ml) water in a large heavy-based saucepan and bring to the boil over high heat. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 3-4 minutes.
